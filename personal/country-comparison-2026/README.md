**Japan + Philippines wins on every axis:**
- **Cost**: $51k/yr (cheapest hybrid)
- **FIRE age**: 56 (1 year FASTER than Japan-only at 57)
- **Flight**: 4 hrs Tokyo↔Manila (closest)
- **Visa**: SRRV — easiest in Asia ($10k deposit, 20-30 day approval)
- **Language**: English (official) — no daily-life tax
- **Weather**: PH dry season Dec-May aligns perfectly with Japan winter escape

**Bali is the trap** — its WET season is exactly Nov-Apr, when you'd be there as a winter escape. Inverse-pair (Japan winter, Bali summer) defeats the seasonal logic. Skip Bali for this play.

Hybrid math: 6 mo each + 2-3 round-trip flights + transit. Stay <180 days/yr in each country = no tax residency anywhere extra.

## Flexible retirement framework

The plan IS the flexibility, not the calendar. Build optionality with financial cushion.

**Why flexibility matters:**
- Life shifts (family, kids, parents' health) — your year-5 calendar won't be year-1's
- Honeymoon vs. settle: how a place feels in week 2 ≠ year 2
- New options emerge (visa programs change, countries open up)
- Risk pivots (currency, political, healthcare drift)

**Flexibility guardrails — preserve optionality:**
- **Don't buy property** — rent in both bases for 3-5 yrs minimum
- **Stack visa options** — TW citizenship + PH SRRV + JP visa-free + TH LTR (if needed)
- **Cash buffer 1-2 yrs** — pivot without forced asset sales
- **No business commitments** — skip Japan Business Manager, skip Vietnam DT investor
- **Annual review** — every October, recalibrate

**Financial safety net — already baked in:**
- **2× spend buffer @ 60** in SAFE mode = ~$100k+ cushion at 401k unlock
- **3× spend buffer @ 67** in SAFE mode = ~$150k cushion at SS claim
- **±20% spend tolerance** — sensitivity analysis confirms the plan absorbs 20% spend swings without breaking
- **9 country options** — always a cheaper or different country available
- **TW citizenship = ultimate fallback** — $42k/yr base, NHI, no visa, no friction. The "everything fails" parachute.

**Bottom line:** the seasonal calendar is the starting hypothesis. Year 1 is data collection. By year 3 you'll know the real shape.

## Methodology

- Calc engine: same `calc/fireCalculator.js#solveFireAge` that powers the dashboard's on-screen verdict. SAFE mode.
- Cost of living: 2026 figures synthesized from Numbeo, Expatistan, Pacific Prime, International Living, Asia Lifestyle Magazine, retirement-visa research blogs. Targets upper-middle-class lifestyle for a couple post-college (kids grown by FIRE age).
- Healthcare: local public + supplementary private (your preference). NHI in Taiwan/Japan; ACA/Medicare in US; IPMI in Singapore; private hospital networks elsewhere.
- Excluded: international school for kids (already modeled by the engine), currency risk, geopolitical risk, visa policy drift.
